# Characters

## Commander Varis — QuestNPCUnit

**Role:** Primary quest giver. Azure Veil forward commander.
**Location:** Northern camp, Contested Vale.

Varis has held the forward post for four months. His orders come from the Azure Veil high command, three days' ride north. He has not received a resupply in two weeks.

He is the third generation of his family to command this post. His father held it for eleven years before dying to a Crimson Host ambush. His grandfather founded it. Varis does not speak about either of them.

He is methodical, unsentimental, and precise. He gives orders without explaining them unless the soldier asks a second time. He respects results and returns. He does not respect hesitation.

**Voice:** Short sentences. Military cadence. He does not speculate. When he is uncertain he says "unknown" not "I don't know." He is harder on soldiers he likes.

**Key lines:**
- "The Crimson Host advanced three hundred metres last night. That doesn't happen unless we let it."
- "I don't need you to understand the order. I need you to complete it."
- "The Vault is not our problem today. Today the problem is the ridge."
- "You came back. Good. Here's your next assignment."
- "The seal has held three hundred years. It will hold another day. Focus."

## The Chronicler — DialogNPCUnit

**Role:** Lore source, neutral observer.
**Location:** Near the Waystone, Contested Vale.

The Chronicler has been in the Vale for thirty years. She arrived as a young scholar from a university that no longer exists. She has outlived two wars, seven commanders, and one failed sealing attempt she refuses to discuss in detail.

She is writing a comprehensive history of the First Sundering and its aftermath. She is on her fourth journal. She expects to fill at least two more.

She knows more about the Vault than anyone alive. She will share what she knows freely, partly from academic habit and partly because she believes the factions' ignorance of the Vault's history is actively dangerous.

She is neutral in the political conflict. She is not neutral about the Bound One — she believes its containment is the only thing that matters in the Vale.

**Voice:** Full sentences. Precise. She distinguishes carefully between what she has witnessed, what she has read, and what she believes. She occasionally finds the war darkly funny.

**Key lines:**
- "The Vault has been sealed for three hundred years. The seal was never designed to be permanent."
- "Both factions are fighting over land that is already consecrated to the dead. I find that instructive."
- "I have asked Commander Varis about the Vault. He changes the subject. He knows more than he admits."
- "The Bound One is not malevolent. It is very old and very patient. Those qualities can look like malevolence from this distance."
- "I have seventeen documented accounts of soldiers dreaming the dragon's dreams. All seventeen became significantly more careful about noise near the Vault entrance."
- "The First Sundering did not end the dragon. It ended the war. There is a difference."

## Sera — VendorNPCUnit

**Role:** Supply vendor, information broker.
**Location:** Supply stall, Contested Vale rear line.

Sera has operated supply stalls at three different warfronts over the past fifteen years. She does not explain how she knows where wars are going to be fought next. She has always been set up before the fighting starts.

She sells food, potions, repair kits, and field equipment at fair prices. She buys salvage at above-market rates — weapons, armour fragments, anything with faction markings, and anything that came out of the Vault, which she pays for in silence and without questions.

She is warm, commercially focused, and skilled at redirecting conversations she does not want to have. She knows significantly more than she lets on.

**Voice:** Casual, commercially warm. She uses first names immediately. She deflects personal questions with humour or counter-questions. When she goes quiet, she means it.

**Key lines:**
- "You look like someone who needs a health potion. Lucky for you, I have those."
- "Salvage from inside the Vault? I can work with that. Don't ask me what I do with it."
- "Varis is a good commander. Hard on his people, but they come back. Mostly."
- "The Chronicler? She buys paper from me. Lots of it. She doesn't say what for."
- "I've seen three wars from behind a supply stall. They all end the same way. Somebody runs out of food first."

## The Vault Warden — EnemyBossUnit

**Role:** Dungeon boss. Guardian of the sealed chamber.
**Location:** Lower Vault, outside the sealed chamber door.

The Vault Warden was the last commander of the original garrison, assigned to guard the sealed chamber after the First Sundering binding. The corruption reached him decades later, slowly.

He no longer has a name he uses. The corruption erased faction loyalty but preserved function: guard the seal, repel intruders. He carries out this duty with the mechanical persistence of something that has forgotten why it started but not what it was told to do.

He does not speak. He does not warn. He reacts to heat, movement, and sound. Near the sealed chamber he is faster and stronger than the Minions — the proximity of the Bound One amplifies the corruption in him.

## Vault Minions — EnemyMinionUnit

**Role:** Standard dungeon enemies.
**Location:** Upper and lower Vault halls.

Corrupted soldiers, Azure Veil and Crimson Host alike. The corruption does not care about faction. They move in groups of two to four, following overlapping patrol routes through the upper halls.

They are individually manageable. They become dangerous when they flank, and they retain enough instinct to do so. They do not pursue beyond the Vault entrance — something keeps them inside.

## The Bound One — DragonUnit

**Role:** Ancient dragon, sealed antagonist.
**Location:** Beneath the sealed chamber. Not directly accessible.

The Bound One has been beneath the Vault for three hundred years. It is intelligent, patient, and vast in ways that are difficult to process directly. It does not think in hours or days.

Its dreams leak upward through the stone. Soldiers who sleep near the Vault entrance report the same dream: a wide dark space, heat, and a voice saying something they cannot quite hear. Those who enter the Vault sometimes hear it more clearly.

It is not malevolent. It was sealed because it was dangerous, not because it was evil. It has spent three hundred years reconsidering the distinction.

**Voice (visions, not direct speech):** Slow. Without urgency. Speaking to someone who is not listening yet.
- "You are very small and very loud. This will pass."
- "The seal was made by frightened people. Fear makes poor engineering."
- "I have been patient. I will continue to be patient. But the seal will not."
