# Quests

## Clear the Advance — KillThingsQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis
**Objective:** Eliminate Crimson Host forward units (RedFighter, RedWarrior, RedArcher, RedMage) in the contested zone.

The Crimson Host advanced three hundred metres overnight. They are establishing a position at the old grain storage ruins at the Vale's centre — a location that would split the Azure Veil's supply line if they fortify it.

Varis needs the position cleared before dawn. He assigns the task without ceremony. He expects the soldier back within the hour.

**Narrative context:**
This is the player's introduction to active combat in the Vale. The grain ruins are a natural chokepoint — good cover, clear sight lines, and close enough to both camps to be genuinely contested. The Red faction soldiers at this position are not the Host's best; they are the advance element, sent to probe and hold.

**Post-completion:** Varis acknowledges completion with a nod. He immediately assigns the next task. He does not celebrate.

## Salvage the Fallen — CollectionQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis (directed to Sera)
**Objective:** Collect salvage items dropped during recent fighting.

Azure Veil equipment is expensive and supply lines are compromised. Weapons, armour fragments, and field kits left on the battlefield are wasted resources. Varis has issued a standing order: recover what can be recovered.

Sera at the supply stall accepts the salvage. She keeps her own records. She pays fairly and does not ask where specific items came from.

**Narrative context:**
This quest encourages exploration of the battlefield areas and interaction with Sera. It gives the player reason to move through the contested zone between the two camps and to visit the supply stall.

**Sera's response on completion:** "Good. These are in better shape than I expected. Come back when you find more — especially anything that came out of the Vault. Different rates for that."

## Consult the Chronicler — DialogObjectiveQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis
**Objective:** Speak with the Chronicler at the Waystone.

Varis needs to know whether the Vault can be safely scouted before committing soldiers. He distrusts the Chronicler's neutrality but acknowledges she is the only person in the Vale who has studied the Vault systematically.

He gives this task reluctantly: "Find the woman at the Waystone. Ask what she knows about the Vault's upper levels. Do not let her talk for more than an hour."

**Narrative context:**
This is the primary lore delivery quest. The Chronicler provides structured information about the Vault's layout, the Minions, the Warden, and — if the player asks — the Bound One.

**The Chronicler's opening:** "Varis sent you. That means he's considering going in. He wouldn't ask unless he was running out of other options. Sit down."

**Key information the Chronicler shares:**
1. The upper halls are accessible but patrolled. Minions move in groups of three.
2. The Vault Warden is in the lower level. Do not engage it without preparation.
3. The seal is cracked in at least three places. She has sketches.
4. The Bound One is not aggressive yet. "Yet" is the operative word.
5. Both factions have sent scouts before. None returned full reports.

## Descend to the Vault — VisitZoneQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis
**Objective:** Enter FeaturesDemoDungeon and reach the first interior checkpoint.

"Intelligence only. Do not engage the Warden. Do not touch the seal. Come back with a map."

Varis needs someone who will actually return. This is a scouting mission, not an assault. The objective is to confirm that the upper halls are accessible and to map the patrol patterns of the Minions.

**Narrative context:**
This is the zone transition quest — it moves the player into FeaturesDemoDungeon. The Vault entrance is at the eastern edge of the Vale. The approach is quiet. The inside is not.

**On entry:** The air is cold. Torches mounted in iron brackets still burn — they should not, after three centuries, but they do. The Minions do not react to light.

**What the player sees:**
- Ordered rows of empty weapon racks
- Collapsed sleeping quarters, dust-covered
- A mess hall with plates still on the tables
- At the far end of the upper hall: a stairwell going down, blocked by rubble except for a gap barely wide enough to pass through

## Activate the Waystone — UseInteractableQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis
**Objective:** Interact with the Waystone at the Vale's centre.

The Waystone is a pre-war teleportation anchor, used by traders to move quickly between market towns. Both factions have avoided activating it because whoever controls it gains a significant tactical advantage.

Varis has decided the advantage is worth the risk. He wants it activated and keyed to the Azure Veil's frequency.

**Narrative context:**
This quest requires the player to cross the contested zone — passing through or around Crimson Host patrol territory — to reach the Waystone. The Chronicler is nearby and may comment as the player approaches.

**The Chronicler's comment on activation:** "That will attract attention. From both sides, and possibly from below. The Waystone is connected to older systems than either faction understands."

## Patrol Duty — RepeatableQuest / Repeatable2StepQuest / Repeatable3StepQuest / Repeatable5StepQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis
**Objective:** Complete patrol checkpoints across the Vale.

Varis rotates soldiers through patrol routes to prevent the Crimson Host from establishing permanent forward positions. This is ongoing work — there are no permanent victories in the Vale, only days that are better than others.

**Patrol Route A (RepeatableQuest):** Single checkpoint. North ridge observation post. Quick turnaround.

**Patrol Route B (Repeatable2StepQuest):** North ridge and eastern Vault perimeter. The eastern leg keeps an eye on unusual Minion activity near the entrance.

**Patrol Route C (Repeatable3StepQuest):** North ridge, Waystone area, Sera's post. Varis wants eyes on all three simultaneously.

**Patrol Route D (Repeatable5StepQuest):** Full perimeter sweep. North ridge, east Vault perimeter, Waystone, south contested zone, back to camp. One hour minimum. Not recommended as a solo exercise.

**Varis's briefing:** "The patrol schedule keeps them guessing. Keep to the route. Report anything unusual. 'Unusual' means anything that wasn't there last time."

## Vault Clearance — FinishAnotherQuestQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis (prerequisite: Descend to the Vault)
**Objective:** Eliminate Vault Minions in the upper Vault halls.

After the scouting report comes back, Varis commits to a partial clearance operation. The upper halls need to be passable before any force can reach the sealed chamber.

"Clear what you can. Do not go past the rubble stairwell. The lower level is not today's problem."

**Narrative context:**
This is the first dungeon combat quest. The Minions in the upper halls move in groups and patrol in overlapping routes. Approaching from behind is effective; approaching head-on is not.

**Encounter design notes:**
- First group: 2 Minions near the entrance
- Second group: 3 Minions near the mess hall
- Third group: 2 Minions near the stairwell, plus one stationary at the top

## Varis's Favour — ChooseRewardQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis
**Objective:** Complete a series of assigned tasks and choose a reward.

Varis has a limited budget for commendations. He offers three options to soldiers who have proven themselves: a combat ration kit (consumable items), a requisitioned weapon upgrade (equipment), or a written commendation to Azure Veil command (faction reputation).

"Take your pick. Don't take long about it."

---

## Training Orders — StatusEffectQuest / UseAbilityQuest / LearnAbilityQuest / LearnTradeSkillQuest

**Quest giver:** Commander Varis
**Objective:** Demonstrate combat readiness before Vault assignment.

Varis will not send undertrained soldiers into the Vault. These quests require the player to demonstrate specific abilities — applying a status effect in combat, using a class ability, learning a skill from a trainer, or demonstrating a trade skill.

**Varis's reasoning:** "The Vault kills soldiers who hesitate. You will not hesitate if you know what you're doing. Demonstrate it."

**Trainers in the Vale:**
- Class trainers are at Varis's camp (use existing ClassTrainer unit profiles)
- Sera can direct the player to skill trainers
- The Chronicler can teach Vault-specific knowledge (DialogObjective variant)

## The Chronicler's Request — DialogIntroductionQuest

**Quest giver:** The Chronicler
**Objective:** Help the Chronicler document the current state of the Vale by completing a task she assigns.

The Chronicler needs a first-hand account of something she cannot observe herself — the interior of the Vault, the behaviour of the Minions up close, the condition of the seal's outer door — and she will trade information for it.

"I have thirty years of history here and a blind spot forty metres underground. Help me fill it and I will tell you things that might keep you alive."

**What she offers in return:** A detailed account of the First Sundering, the original binding ritual, and why the seal is failing. This is the highest-tier lore in the game.
